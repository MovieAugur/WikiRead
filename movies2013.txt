Three popular films (''[[Top Gun]]'', ''[[Jurassic Park (film)|Jurassic Park]]'', and ''[[The Wizard of Oz (1939 film)|The Wizard of Oz]]'') were re-released in [[3D film|3D]] and [[IMAX]].

''[[Frozen (2013 film)|Frozen]]'' and ''[[Iron Man 3]]'' both grossed over $1.2 billion, making them the fifth and sixth [[List of highest-grossing films|highest-grossing films]] of all time, and the eighteenth and sixteenth films to surpass the billion dollar mark, respectively. ''Frozen'' became the second animated film after ''[[Toy Story 3]]'' to gross $1 billion, and is the [[List of highest-grossing animated films|highest-grossing animated film]] of all time worldwide. After being re-released in 3-D, ''[[Jurassic Park (film)|Jurassic Park]]'' reached a new milestone: it is the seventeenth film to have surpassed the $1 billion mark, with $1.029 billion, and is the fifteen highest-grossing film of all time. It is the second time that twelve films have earned more than $500 million worldwide (''[[The Croods]]'' and ''[[World War Z (film)|World War Z]]'' also grossed over $500 million), and also the third time in which two films released by the same studio have grossed over $1 billion (''Frozen'' and ''Iron Man 3''), with this instance being [[Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures|Disney]]'s second time (the other being [[2010 in film|2010]]).
