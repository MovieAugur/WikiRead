The year '''2003 in film''' involved some significant events. Releases of sequels took place with movies like ''[[The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King]]'', ''[[2 Fast 2 Furious]]'', ''[[Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle]]'', ''[[The Matrix Reloaded]]'', ''[[The Matrix Revolutions]]'', ''[[Pokémon Heroes]]'', ''[[Terminator 3: Rise of the Machines]]'', ''[[Freddy vs. Jason]]'', ''[[X2 (film)|X2: X-Men United]]'', ''[[Bad Boys II]]'', ''[[Scary Movie 3]]'', and ''[[Final Destination 2]]''.

''[[The Lord of the Rings: The Return of the King]]'' grossed more than $1.19&nbsp;billion, making it the highest-grossing film in 2003 worldwide and in North America and (at the time) [[List of highest-grossing films|the second-highest-grossing film]] of all time in North America. It is now, unadjusted, the sixth-highest-grossing film of all time.

''[[Finding Nemo]]'' was the highest grossing animated movie of all time but it has since been overtaken by ''[[Shrek 2]]'' in 2004, ''[[Ice Age: Dawn of the Dinosaurs]]'' in 2009, ''[[Toy Story 3]]'' in 2010 and the [[Digital 3D|3D]] rerelease of ''[[The Lion King]]'' in 2011, leaving it currently the [[List of highest-grossing animated films|5th highest grossing animated film]] of all time and now the 3rd highest-grossing animated film after its 3D release and the [[List of highest grossing films|25th highest grossing film]] of all time (it was once the 9th highest grossing film).

==Events==
* February 24 - ''[[The Pianist (2002 film)|The Pianist]]'', directed by [[Roman Polanski]], wins 7 [[César Award]]s: Best Film, Best Director, Best Actor, Best Sound, Best Production Design, Best Music and Best Cinematography.
* November 17: [[Arnold Schwarzenegger]] sworn in as [[Governor of California]].
* December 22: Both of the movies from the [[Matrix trilogy]] released in 2003 were shut out of [[visual effects]] [[Academy Awards|Oscar]] consideration by the [[Visual Effects Award Nominating Committee]].
* December 31 The [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] mails nomination ballots in which it qualifies 254 films released in 2003 as eligible for [[Academy Awards|Oscar]] consideration.
