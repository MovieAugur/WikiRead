| 4 ||The [[Producers Guild of America]] nominates ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'', ''[[Capote (film)|Capote]]'', ''[[Good Night, and Good Luck]]'', ''[[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]]'' and ''[[Walk the Line]]'' as contenders for their best-produced film award. The [[Writers Guild of America]] (WGA) nominate ''[[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]]'', ''[[Good Night, and Good Luck]]'', ''[[Bone Thugs-n-Harmony#I Tried (The Bone Thugs Movie)|I Tried]]'', ''[[The 40-Year-Old Virgin]]'' and ''[[The Squid and the Whale]]'' for best original screenplay. The WGA nominees for best adapted screenplay are ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'', ''[[Capote (film)|Capote]]'', ''[[The Constant Gardener (film)|The Constant Gardener]]'', ''[[A History of Violence (film)|A History of Violence]]'' and ''[[Syriana]]''.

|- valign="top"
| 9 ||The [[Broadcast Film Critics Association]] present their [[BFCA Critics' Choice Awards 2005|Critics' Choice Awards]] for the best films of 2005 live on [[The WB]] network in the United States. ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'' is named best picture, best director for [[Ang Lee]] and ties for best supporting actress for [[Michelle Williams (actor)|Michelle Williams]]. [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]] is named best actor for ''[[Capote (film)|Capote]]'' and [[Reese Witherspoon]] is awarded best actress for ''[[Walk the Line]]''.
|- valign="top"
| 16 ||The winners of the [[63rd Golden Globe Awards]] include ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'' for best dramatic picture and best director.

| 28 ||The [[Directors Guild of America]] names [[Ang Lee]] best film director of 2005 for ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'', best documentary goes to [[Werner Herzog]] for ''[[Grizzly Man]]'' and its Lifetime Achievement Award goes to [[Clint Eastwood]].
|- valign="top"
| 29 ||The [[Screen Actors Guild]] names [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]] outstanding male movie actor for ''[[Capote (film)|Capote]]'', [[Reese Witherspoon]] as outstanding female lead movie actor for ''[[Walk the Line]]'', [[Rachel Weisz]] as outstanding female actor in a supporting role for ''[[The Constant Gardener (film)|The Constant Gardener]]'', [[Paul Giamatti]] as outstanding male actor in a supporting role for ''[[Cinderella Man]]'' the cast of ''[[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]]'' as outstanding ensemble in a theatrical motion picture, and [[Shirley Temple Black]] is given a life achievement award.
|- valign="top"
| 30 ||The [[2005 Golden Raspberry Awards|26th Annual Golden Raspberry Awards]] nominees include ''[[Deuce Bigalow: European Gigolo]]'', ''[[Dirty Love (film)|Dirty Love]]'', ''[[The Dukes of Hazzard (film)|The Dukes of Hazzard]]'', ''[[House of Wax (2005 film)|House of Wax]]'' and ''[[Son of the Mask]]'' for worst film; [[Tom Cruise]], [[Will Ferrell]], [[Jamie Kennedy]], [[Dwayne Johnson|The Rock]] and [[Rob Schneider]] for worst actor; and, [[Jessica Alba]], [[Hilary Duff]], [[Jennifer Lopez]], [[Jenny McCarthy]] and [[Tara Reid]] for worst actress.
|- valign="top"
| 31 ||The [[Academy Awards]] for best film achievement in 2005 had nominated primarily independent films. ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]'' led the nominations with 8, followed by ''[[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]]'', ''[[Good Night, and Good Luck]]'' and ''[[Memoirs of a Geisha (film)|Memoirs of a Geisha]]'' all earning six.
|- valign="top"
|rowspan="2"| February
| 5 ||The 33rd annual [[Annie Award]] - ''[[Wallace and Gromit|Wallace and Gromit: The Curse of the Were-Rabbit]]'' won the best animated feature, as well as all nine categories which it was nominated. ''[[Family Guy]]'' won the best voice acting and directing, ''[[Star Wars|Star Wars: Clone Wars II Chapters 21-25]]'' won the best animated TV production, ''[[Lilo and Stitch|Lilo and Stitch 2: Stitch has a Glitch]]'' won the best home entertainment award, and ''[[Ultimate Spider-Man]]'' won the new "best video game award".
|- valign="top"
| 6 ||Disney re-acquires the rights to [[Oswald the Lucky Rabbit]] from [[NBCUniversal]], in exchange for ABC letting commentator [[Al Michaels]] work on ''[[NBC Sunday Night Football]]''.

|rowspan="3"| March
| 4 || ''[[Dirty Love (film)|Dirty Love]]'' dominates the [[26th Golden Raspberry Awards]] with 4 awards including Worst Picture and Worst Actress for [[Jenny McCarthy]]. [[Rob Schneider]] took home for Worst Actor for his performance in ''[[Deuce Bigalow: European Gigolo]]'' Other awards went to [[Hayden Christensen]] as Worst Supporting Actor for ''[[Star Wars Episode III: Revenge of the Sith]]'' and [[Paris Hilton]] as Worst Supporting Actress for ''[[House of Wax (2005 film)|House of Wax]]''
|- valign="top"
| 5 || [[78th Academy Awards]]: ''[[Crash (2004 film)|Crash]]'' earns a win for [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]] upsetting favorite ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]''. No film, for the first time in 58 years, won a clear majority. Both films as well as ''[[Memoirs of a Geisha (film)|Memoirs of a Geisha]]'' and ''[[King Kong (2005 film)|King Kong]]'' win three [[Oscars]] each. Favorite ''[[March of the Penguins]]'' wins Oscar for [[Academy Award for Documentary Feature|Documentary Feature]]. South Africa wins its first motion picture Oscar with the Best Foreign Film award for ''[[Tsotsi]]''. ''[[Wallace and Gromit: The Curse of the Were-Rabbit]]'' wins Oscar for [[Academy Award for Best Animated Feature|Animated feature film]].

Major awards as follows:
*[[Academy Award for Best Director|Director]]: [[Ang Lee]] for ''[[Brokeback Mountain]]''
*[[Academy Award for Best Actor|Actor]]: [[Philip Seymour Hoffman]] for ''[[Capote (film)|Capote]]''
*[[Academy Award for Best Actress|Actress]]: [[Reese Witherspoon]] for ''[[Walk the Line]]''
*[[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor|Supporting Actor]]: [[George Clooney]] for ''[[Syriana]]''
*[[Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress|Supporting Actress]]: [[Rachel Weisz]] for ''[[The Constant Gardener (film)|The Constant Gardener]]''
