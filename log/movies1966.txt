The year '''1966 in film''' involved some significant events.

==Events==
Entertainment pioneer [[Walt Disney]], best known for his creation of [[Mickey Mouse]], breakthroughs in the field of [[animation]] and [[theme park]] design and other extraordinary achievements, died on 15 December 1966 at the age of sixty-five of acute [[circulatory collapse]] following a diagnosis of, and surgery for, lung cancer.  He had been a smoker since the 1940s. He died while he was producing ''[[The Jungle Book (1967 film)|The Jungle Book]]'', ''[[The Happiest Millionaire]]'', and ''[[Winnie the Pooh and the Blustery Day]]''; the last three films under his personal supervision.
